History of jigsaw puzzles

Jigsaw puzzles have a fascinating history dating back to the 18th century.

They originated in Europe, specifically in England, around the 1760s.

Initially, they were created as educational tools to teach geography

and were called "dissected maps".

These early puzzles were made of wood and handcrafted.

The concept evolved, and by the early 19th century, puzzles began featuring

artistic scenes and became popular pastimes for adults.

They were crafted from various materials, including paper and cardboard,

and were known as "jigsaw puzzles" due to the tool used to

cut them into intricate pieces -though they actually used a fretsaw, the name jigsaw

was adopted.

In the 20th century, advances in printing technology allowed for mass production of puzzles,

making them more affordable and accessible.

Puzzles with a wide range of themes and complexities emerged, catering to diverse interests and age groups.

Today, jigsaw puzzles continue to be a beloved form of entertainment and relaxation.

They have also found their place in the digital world, with online versions for enthusiasts.

Jigsaw puzzles remain a timeless and enduring form of amusement that has captivated generations worldwide.

Puzzles are amazing!
